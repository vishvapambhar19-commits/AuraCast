About Air Pollutants

PM2.5

▼

Fine particulate matter from sources like smoke and vehicle emissions. These tiny particles can penetrate deep into the lungs and enter the bloodstream, causing respiratory and cardiovascular problems.

Ozone (O₃)

▼

A gas created by chemical reactions with sunlight. Ground-level ozone is a major component of smog and can cause chest pain, coughing, and throat irritation, especially on hot, sunny days.

Nitrogen Dioxide (NO₂)

▼

A gas formed from high-temperature combustion, primarily from vehicle emissions and power plants. It can irritate the respiratory system and contributes to the formation of other pollutants.
